This is my webpage for this course in 2000-2001 when I only taught it in the second and third term.

What we have done so far.

These page numbers refer to the 8th edition of Kreyszig. The differential equations material is taken from Chapter 3, the series solutions from Chapter 4, the vector calculus from Chapter 8 and the integral calculus from Chapter 9.

Differential Equations Mixing problems (K 152-154, PS 1 q 1). Linear homogenous first order equations, definitions and existence of solutions (K 160). Solutions and plotting solutions (K 162-167, PS 1 q 2 and 3, PS 2, note 5). A linear system with a repeated eigenvalue (K 167-169, PS 3 q 1). Converting a second order linear homogeneous equation to a first order system (K 156-158, PS 3 q 3 and 4, note 2 below). The pendulum as an example of linearization (K 176-177). The pendulum with air resistence (K 177-178). Other examples of linearization (PS 4, note 3, PS 5 q 1), more on centers (K 166). Inhomogeneous equations (K 187, PS 5 q 2, PS 6).

Series solutions Solving differential equations with series solutions (K 194-205, PS 7 and 8 q 1 and 2). The Legendre polynomials (K 205, PS8 q3).

Vector calculus Revision of vectors (K 400-422, PS 9). Curves and the derivative of a vector (K 425-426), length along a curve (K 432, PS10, q1, PS11 q1). Grad (K 446-450, PS 10), div (K 453-456 PS 10 q 2, PS 11 q 2 and 3), the continuity equation (K 454-455), the Laplacian (K 451, PS 10 q 2 and 4, PS 11 q 3) and curl (K 457-459, PS 11, q 4 and 5).

Integral calculus Line integrals (K464-467, PS12). Motivation, work done by force (K468). Double integrals (K478-482, PS 13 q 1-3). Green's Theorem on the plane (K485-490, PS13 q 4 and 5, PS14 q 1). Gauss Divergence Theorem (K505-509, PS14 q 2, 3 and 6).
