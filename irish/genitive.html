Genitive

Nom. sing. ends with: Gen. sing. ends with: Gender First declension Broad consonant Slender consonant Masculine Second declension Broad or slender consonant -e/-í Feminine with rare exceptions Third declension Slender or broad consonant -a Masculine or feminine Fourth declension Vowel or -ín (no change) Masculine or feminine Fifth declension Vowel or slender consonant Broad consonant Mostly feminine

Singular genitive

1st declension: broad masculine

make the word slender.

bád -> báid fear -> fir

2nd declension: end in consonant and feminine

make the word slender if it isn't and add a -e or sometimes an -í.

bróg -> bróige stráid -> stráide

3rd declenson: -óir, -éir, áil, úil, -acht, among others

make the word broad if it isn't and add a -a.

ceacht -> ceachta feoil -> feola

4th declenson: end in a vowel or -ín

No change.

cailín -> cailín bia -> bia

5th declenson: mostly feminine, slender or ending in a vowel

Change the end to a broad consonant, either suffixed or formed by broadening, or both. An unstressed short vowel may be dropped before l or r when a suffix is added; often irregular

abhainn -> abhann cathair -> cathrach

Articles and the genitive in the singular

Masculine

The aricle is an: adds a t before s and lenites appropriate consonants except d and t. A t is not prefixed before vowels. an siopa -> fear an tsiopa an cailín -> ainm an chailín an duine -> nuacht an duine an t-uisce -> blas an uisce

Feminine

The article is na - adds a h before vowels, otherwise the word is not mutated. an tsaoire -> lá na saoire an bhráillín -> dáth na bráillín an oiche -> lár na hoiche

Plural genitive

Gentives are simpler in the plural, for all declensions except the first they are the same as the common plural form. In the first declension the genetive plural is the same as the common singular.

Common Genitive Singular bád báid Plural báid bád

This doesn't apply when the plural is formed with a suffix, unless the suffix is -a

Some 2nd declension nouns, mostly ones for body parts, use the common singular as the genitive.

lámh cluas cnámh cos bos bróg

Article and the genitive in the plural

This is easy, it is just na and eclipsis, with an n- before nouns.

caint na ndaoine

Double genitive

Only the last noun is in the genitive, but lenite any noun acting as a possessor: obair bhean an tí.
